About the Poet

Abubakar Saadu Olusola, known by his pen name(Prince Haybee), is a graduate of the University of Ilorin, where he earned a B.Ed in English. His passion for poetry began in his secondary school days and flourished during his 100-level year at Unilorin with the creation of his first poem, The Hidden Lake. Since then, he has written over 60 poems and still counting. He continues to craft verses that spread love, happiness, and reflections on societal and worldly happenings. Prince Haybee is driven by a desire to inspire change, using his words to echo truths that resonate with humanity. His poetry transcends boundaries, challenging perspectives, comforting hearts, and calling for a better world. Through his works, he seeks to connect with readers on a deeply personal level, shining a light on the beauty and complexity of life, love, and the society we share. He is a poet and truth-seeker dedicated to using words as a force for justice, healing, and change. His poetry explores the struggles and triumphs of humanity from the plight of the common people to reflections on inner battles and faith.

Poetry-Haven was born from the belief that poetry can be both a refuge and a revolution. This space is a home for truth-telling, emotional honesty, and soul-searching. Each poem here speaks to a deeper part of life. Calling readers to reflect, feel, and rise.
