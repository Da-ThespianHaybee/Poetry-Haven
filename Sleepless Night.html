Sleepless Night

By Prince Haybee

Night life!
Silence swallows the Earth.
Retiring to my bed,
My eyes feel reluctant to close.

Is this progress?
My mind ponders in filth.
Rolling round and round,
My body freezes to roll around.

Rising uncountable times,
A Restroom seems cooler than the Rest-rooms.
Sleeping seems not sleepy but feels Sleepy.
This is going to be a long night, says the minds with dreams.

Hoping for a better tomorrow,
I pray to wake up not...
in a six feet, below the ground.

Poem Synopsis

Sleepless Night captures the restless agony of a mind burdened by anxiety, uncertainty, and fear of the unknown. The speaker portrays the stillness of the night not as peace but as a haunting reminder of internal struggles. Wrestling with sleeplessness, they long for survival and better days ahead, fearing death’s quiet approach. It is a deeply emotional portrayal of sleepless nights faced by dreamers, worriers, and those caught in life's turmoil.

Themes

  • Anxiety and Restlessness
  • Fear of Death and the Unknown
  • Hope and Prayer
  • Isolation in Darkness
  • Struggle for a Better Future

Poetic Devices

  • Personification: “Silence swallows the Earth.”
  • Repetition: “Rolling round and round.”
  • Alliteration: “Restroom seems cooler than Rest-rooms.”
  • Irony: The irony between 'restrooms' and true rest.
  • Oxymoron: “Sleeping seems not sleepy but feels Sleepy.”

Tone & Mood

Tone: Reflective, troubled, somber.
Mood: Tense, anxious, melancholic.

Structure & Rhyme Pattern

The poem follows a free verse structure with no consistent rhyme scheme, reflecting the disorder of an anxious mind. Some internal rhymes and slant rhymes add subtle rhythm without imposing order.

Societal and Life Connection

This poem echoes the silent suffering of individuals dealing with internal struggles. In a world weighed down by economic strain, pressure to succeed, and emotional trauma, many people experience this very torment. It speaks for the dreamers who battle their thoughts nightly and the fear of what tomorrow might bring.
